Our Team

Meet the talented researchers and staff of the Ji Laboratory

Leadership

James (Hong-Long) Ji, MD, PhD

Professor of Surgery | Principal Investigator

Dr. Ji leads a multidisciplinary research team focused on understanding the molecular mechanisms of pulmonary diseases, with particular emphasis on acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), COVID-19, and sarcoidosis. His research employs cutting-edge approaches including multi-omics analysis, humanized mouse models, and iPSC-derived organoids to identify new therapeutic targets and biomarkers.

Education:

  • MD, Xinxiang Medical University
  • PhD, Molecular Medicine, University of Kent
  • Postdoctoral Fellowship, Pulmonary Physiology, University of Georgia

Research Interests:

  • Lung injury and repair mechanisms
  • Epithelial sodium channels (ENaC) in pulmonary edema
  • Surfactant protein regulation of alveolar epithelial cell fate
  • Host-pathogen interactions in respiratory diseases
  • Multi-omics approaches to biomarker discovery

Hobbies: Gardening, Hiking, Travel, Photography, Exercise

PhD Student - Computer Science

Arslan is a PhD student in Computer Science at Loyola University Chicago, where he focuses on using AI to tackle real-world challenges. His work explores how artificial intelligence can help detect cyberbullying, fight misinformation, improve cybersecurity, and support biomedical research.
