Join Our Team

Opportunities to work with us on cutting-edge pulmonary research

Why Join the Ji Laboratory?

Our lab offers a dynamic and collaborative environment where innovative research in pulmonary diseases takes place. We are dedicated to understanding the molecular mechanisms of ARDS, COVID-19, and sarcoidosis using cutting-edge approaches.

As a member of our team, you will:

  • Work with state-of-the-art technology and methodologies, including scRNAseq, multi-omics approaches, and humanized mouse models
  • Collaborate with scientists across disciplines and institutions
  • Develop valuable skills in molecular biology, cell physiology, and computational analysis
  • Contribute to research with direct clinical implications for respiratory diseases
  • Be part of a supportive and inclusive research community

"Our laboratory is committed to fostering the next generation of scientists. We provide mentorship, resources, and opportunities for growth in a collaborative environment focused on scientific excellence and translational impact."

- Dr. James (Hong-Long) Ji, Principal Investigator

Current Opportunities

Information about joining our research team:

Postdoctoral Fellows

Status: No Available Positions

We currently do not have any available postdoctoral positions. We operate with a small team of postdoctoral researchers who are focused on our funded projects. We will update this page when new funding becomes available.

Typical Qualifications:
  • PhD in Molecular Biology, Cell Biology, Physiology, or related field
  • Strong background in molecular and cellular biology techniques
  • Experience with mouse models and/or organoid culture
  • Publication record in peer-reviewed journals
  • Excellent communication and teamwork skills

Please check back in the future for updates on available positions.

Graduate Students

Status: Self-Funded Only

We welcome self-funded graduate students who are interested in our research areas. If you have your own fellowship or scholarship, we can discuss potential projects that align with your interests and our lab's focus.

Requirements:
  • B.S. or M.S. in Biology, Biochemistry, or related field
  • Strong academic record
  • Independent funding source (fellowship, scholarship, etc.)
  • Research interests that align with our lab's focus areas
  • Motivation and commitment to scientific research
Contact Dr. Ji

Undergraduate Students

Status: Self-Funded Only

We accept motivated undergraduate students who can secure their own funding through university programs, external scholarships, or volunteer positions. These opportunities provide valuable hands-on experience in a research environment.

Requirements:
  • Current enrollment at Loyola University Chicago or partner institutions
  • Completion of basic biology and chemistry courses
  • Self-funding through scholarships or willingness to volunteer
  • Minimum commitment of 10 hours per week for at least one semester
  • Enthusiasm for scientific research
Contact Dr. Ji

Funding Notice

Our laboratory operates primarily through grant funding which limits our ability to accept new team members. Currently, we can only accommodate self-funded graduate and undergraduate students.

If you're interested in our research and have your own funding source, please contact Dr. Ji directly to discuss potential opportunities.

Application Process

For self-funded students interested in joining our team:

1

Prepare Your Materials

Assemble the following documents:

  • CV or resume
  • Cover letter describing your research interests, relevant experience, and why you want to join our lab
  • Academic transcripts
  • Documentation of your funding source or scholarship
  • Contact information for 1-2 references
2

Contact Dr. Ji

Send your application materials to hji@luc.edu with the subject line "Self-Funded Student Application - [Your Name]"

In your email, clearly indicate:

  • Your academic level (undergraduate or graduate)
  • Your funding source and duration
  • Your availability (start date, hours per week, duration of commitment)
  • Any specific research interests within our lab's focus areas
3

Interview

If your background and interests align with our lab's capacity and research focus, you may be invited for:

  • An initial meeting with Dr. Ji (virtual or in-person)
  • A lab tour and meetings with current lab members
  • Discussion of potential projects based on your interests and skills
4

Decision & Onboarding

If both parties agree to proceed, we will:

  • Confirm scheduling and project details
  • Complete necessary university paperwork
  • Arrange lab safety training and orientation
  • Develop a research and mentoring plan suited to your goals
Important Note:

Due to limited capacity and resources, we can only accept a small number of self-funded students at any given time. Selection is competitive and based on alignment with our current research priorities, your background, and available mentorship capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our laboratory operates primarily through grant funding which is allocated to specific research projects with predetermined staff allocations. At this time, all our funded positions are filled. We can only accommodate additional lab members who bring their own funding through scholarships, fellowships, or are willing to volunteer (undergraduates only).

For undergraduates, potential funding sources include Loyola's LUROP program, work-study positions, summer research programs, or external undergraduate research scholarships. Graduate students may explore university fellowships, national fellowships (NSF, NIH), or scholarships from private foundations related to pulmonary research. International students should check funding opportunities from their home countries.

Undergraduate students from Loyola University Chicago may volunteer in the lab for course credit or experience, pending available mentorship capacity. Due to training requirements and lab safety regulations, we cannot accept volunteers who are not affiliated with Loyola University Chicago.

Funded positions typically become available when new grants are secured or when current lab members complete their projects and move on. The availability of these positions is unpredictable. We will update this page when new funded positions open up. You can also periodically check the Loyola University Chicago job portal for official postings.

Self-funded students typically work on projects that complement our main research directions in lung injury, repair, and host-pathogen interactions. The specific project will depend on your skills, interests, and time commitment. We try to design projects that are manageable within your timeframe while contributing meaningfully to our research goals and providing valuable training for you.

Have Questions?

If you have any questions about opportunities in our lab or the application process, please don't hesitate to contact us. We're happy to discuss potential projects and how your interests might align with our research.
